About Me

I am a researcher and engineer working at the intersection of machine learning, data engineering, and computational geometry. My work focuses on building scalable AI systems for content understanding, recommendations, and semantic search, with applications in manufacturing and applied physics.

Currently, I lead R&D initiatives at Axya (Montréal), where I design and deploy production-scale ML pipelines, multimodal generative systems, and recommendation engines that power supplier discovery and process automation.

Previously, I conducted doctoral research under Prof. Yaoyao Fiona Zhao at McGill University and Prof. Clément Fortin at Skolkovo Institute of Science and Technology.

I have also been a visiting researcher at the Airbus R&T Campus in Toulouse, ISAE-SUPAERO (Department of Complex Systems Engineering, with Prof. Rob Vingerhoets), and C3D Labs, working on model-based engineering, big data in aerospace, and CAD kernel applications.

My broader interests include large-scale machine learning infrastructure, data-centric AI, and generative design systems. For my parametric modeling software for cellular structures, see LatticeQuery.

Energy flows in electric grids

Power in electrical grids is modeled using a set of ordinary differential equations. The system is reduced to static one-dimensional flow, lacking branches, and containing an infinite number of loads. By converting a discrete model into homogeneous ODE's and rescaling ideal boundary conditions to initial conditions, distribution of active and reactive power, and voltage with respect to length are found. Read more.

PIs: Prof. Konstantin Turitsyn, MIT ; Prof. Ildar Gabitov, University of Arizona
